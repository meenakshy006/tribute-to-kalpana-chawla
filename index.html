Kalpana Chawla

1961 - 2003

About

Kalpana Chawla was an astronaut and engineer, the first woman of Indian origin in space. She first flew on Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997 as a mission specialist and primary robotic arm operator. Tragically, she lost her life in the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster in 2003.

  • ☛ Born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, Haryana, India.
  • ☛ Earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from Punjab Engineering College.
  • ☛ Completed her master's and doctorate in aerospace engineering in the USA.
  • ☛ Joined NASA in 1994 and became the first woman of Indian origin in space.
